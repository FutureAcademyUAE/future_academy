Step-by-Step Setup Guide
Follow these 8 steps in order — complete setup takes approximately 3–4 hours
1
Secure your academy Gmail account
Gmail — future.academy.ae@gmail.com · Already active ✓
Your Gmail is the master key for everything. First, enable 2-Step Verification: go to myaccount.google.com → Security → 2-Step Verification. This protects your entire academy. Then create teacher Gmail accounts using the format: math.futureacademy@gmail.com, arabic.futureacademy@gmail.com, etc. One account per teacher keeps everything organised.
💡 Never share your master Gmail password. Only you have full access. Teachers get their own academy Gmail — not yours.
Security Settings → Create Teacher Gmail
2
Create Google Classroom classes
Google Classroom — classroom.google.com
Sign in with your academy Gmail. Click the + button → "Create class". Create one class per subject per grade. Naming formula: "Grade 4 — Mathematics | Future Academy". Add your logo as the class banner photo. Set the class timezone to Gulf Standard Time (UTC+4). Write a class description: "Welcome to Grade 4 Maths at Future Academy. Your teacher is Mr. [Name]. Classes are every Sunday & Tuesday at 9:00 AM UAE time."
💡 Start with 3 pilot classes only: Grade 4 Maths, Grade 4 Arabic, Grade 4 English. Get them perfect before expanding to all grades.
Open Google Classroom →
3
Invite teachers as co-teachers
Google Classroom → People Tab → Invite Teacher
Inside each class, go to the People tab. Under "Teachers", click the person icon and type your teacher's Gmail. Set them as "Co-teacher" — they can post lessons, create assignments, grade work, and start live classes. They do this from Egypt, fully remote. The class owner (you) stays in full control and can remove any teacher at any time.
💡 Only add each teacher to the classes they teach. A Maths teacher should not see the Quran class. This is both professional and secure.
4
Set up YouTube channel for recorded lessons
YouTube Studio — studio.youtube.com
Sign in with your academy Gmail → Click your avatar → Create a channel named "Future Academy أكاديمية المستقبل". Upload your logo as channel icon and cover photo. When teachers record a lesson (via Meet or phone), they send you the video file via Google Drive. You upload it to YouTube as Unlisted (not Public, not Private). Then paste the link into the relevant Google Classroom post. Students click and watch instantly — no YouTube account needed.
💡 "Unlisted" means only people with the exact link can watch. It won't show in YouTube search. This is your secure video library.
YouTube Studio →
5
Configure live class Meet links
Google Meet — Built into Classroom → Settings → Generate Meet link
In each Google Classroom, go to Settings → find "Meet" → turn it on and click "Generate link". This creates a permanent Meet link for that class. Share this link with enrolled students once — they use the same link every single session for that subject, all year. Teachers start the session from Egypt at the scheduled time. Students click the link from UAE and join instantly. The session records automatically if the teacher enables it.
💡 Keep the same Meet link all term. Students will memorise it. Only change if there is a security concern (e.g. unknown person joins).
6
Enrol students with class codes
Google Classroom → Class Settings → Class Code
Each class has a unique 6-character code (e.g. abc123). Share this code with parents via WhatsApp. The parent opens Google Classroom on their child's device, clicks "+", chooses "Join class", and types the code. The student is instantly enrolled. For young students (Grade 1–3), the parent manages the device. Recommended child Gmail format: ahmed.grade4.future@gmail.com.
💡 Create a short welcome PDF for parents explaining how to join. Send it via WhatsApp when they sign up. This reduces confusion and support messages.
7
Build WhatsApp broadcast system
WhatsApp Business — +971502307960
Open WhatsApp Business → New Broadcast → Create one list per grade: "Grade 1 Parents 🎓", "Grade 4 Parents 🎓", etc. Every Sunday morning send a "Weekly Update" to each list. Include: this week's schedule, homework due, any new recorded lessons, and reminders. Important: Parents must save your number for broadcasts to reach them — include this instruction in your welcome message.
💡 Set up 3 Quick Replies for common questions: "What is the class link?", "How do I pay?", "When is the next class?" — saves you hours every week.
8
Set up Google Sheets fee tracker
Google Sheets — sheets.google.com
Create a Google Sheet called "Future Academy — Management 2026". Add 4 tabs: Students, Payments, Attendance, Grades. This is your free CRM system. Mark payment status as: ✅ Paid · ⏳ Due · ❌ Overdue. At the start of each month, filter "Due" students and send payment reminders via WhatsApp. Collect fees by UAE bank transfer (share your Emirates NBD or FAB IBAN). Confirm payment via WhatsApp message.
💡 Offer a 10% discount for full-term payment upfront (3 months). This improves your cash flow and reduces monthly chasing.
Create Tracker → View Payment System

📝 Assignment Types & How to Use Them
📄 Assignment
Students submit written work, photos of handwritten homework, or files. Teacher reviews and gives a grade + comment. Use for: essays, exercises, project reports.
📝 Quiz Assignment
Linked to a Google Form quiz. Auto-grades on submission. Students see their score immediately. Use for: weekly quizzes, vocab tests, maths drills. Results go to your Sheets tracker.
📢 Material
Post a recorded lesson link (YouTube), a PDF worksheet, or a presentation. No submission needed. Use for: sharing recorded lessons, study guides, reading materials.

Live Classes — Google Meet
Professional live teaching from Egypt to UAE — setup, conduct, and recording
📹 Teacher Setup (Egypt Side)
1
Internet: Minimum 10 Mbps upload. Fibre recommended. Test at fast.com
2
Camera: Any laptop webcam works. Ring light = professional look (AED 50)
3
Microphone: Headset mic is better than laptop mic. Any earphones with mic work
4
Background: Plain wall or bookshelf. Use Google Meet's blur background option
5
Start 5 min early: Check camera, mic, and screen share before students join
🛠️ Meet Features to Use Every Class
🖥️ Screen Share — Share your Canva presentation, a PDF worksheet, or your browser. Students see exactly what you see.
✏️ Whiteboard — Click Activities → Whiteboard. Write maths problems, diagrams, Arabic words. Works like a real classroom board.
✋ Raise Hand — Students click the hand icon to signal they want to answer or have a question. Keeps order in large groups.
🔴 Record — Click Activities → Recording → Start recording. The video saves to your Google Drive automatically.
💬 Chat — Students can type questions in the chat without interrupting. Teacher checks between explanations.
📊 Polls — Click Activities → Polls. Ask a quick question and see all answers instantly. Great for checking understanding.
⏱️ Recommended 45-Minute Class Structure
0–5 MIN
Welcome & Recap
Greet students. Review last class. Check homework briefly.
5–30 MIN
New Lesson (Screen Share + Whiteboard)
Teach new content with slides/whiteboard. Ask questions. Use polls to check understanding.
30–42 MIN
Practice & Q&A
Students try exercises. Teacher circulates via chat. Answer questions live.
42–45 MIN
Homework & Close
Assign homework in Classroom. Say goodbye. Stop recording.
